Udayagiri & Khandagiri Caves, Odisha

About Udayagiri & Khandagiri Caves

The Udayagiri and Khandagiri Caves are partly natural and partly artificial caves located in Bhubaneswar, Odisha. These caves have significant historical and religious importance, dating back to the 1st century BCE, during the reign of King Kharavela of the Mahameghavahana dynasty.

They were carved as residential blocks for Jain monks and are adorned with beautiful inscriptions and motifs that reflect the art and culture of ancient Kalinga. Udayagiri (Hill of Sunrise) has 18 caves and Khandagiri has 15, offering a panoramic view of the Bhubaneswar cityscape.

Interesting Facts

  • Udayagiri’s Rani Gumpha is the most famous cave with elaborate sculptures and a two-story design.
  • Many of the caves have inscriptions in Brahmi script.
  • The caves are a pilgrimage site for Jains and are also popular among tourists and history enthusiasts.
  • The site is maintained by the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI).

Best Time to Visit

Winter (October to February) is the best time to explore the caves due to the cool and pleasant climate. Early morning or late afternoon visits offer the best light for photography.

Getting There

The caves are located around 7 km from Bhubaneswar Railway Station and are easily accessible by auto-rickshaw or taxi. The nearest airport is Biju Patnaik International Airport.
