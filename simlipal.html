Simlipal National Park, Odisha

About Simlipal National Park

Simlipal National Park is a wildlife sanctuary and one of India’s top tiger reserves located in the Mayurbhanj district of Odisha. Spread over 2,750 square kilometers, it is part of the UNESCO World Network of Biosphere Reserves and is rich in biodiversity, with dense forests, scenic waterfalls, and diverse wildlife.

Named after the red silk cotton trees (Simul), the park is home to Bengal tigers, Asian elephants, leopards, and a wide variety of bird and reptile species. The lush landscape and tranquil environment make it a perfect retreat for eco-tourism and adventure lovers.

Interesting Facts

  • It houses two stunning waterfalls—Barehipani and Joranda, both over 150 meters high.
  • Simlipal is one of the few tiger reserves in India where melanistic (black) tigers have been spotted.
  • The park hosts over 1,000 species of plants and nearly 100 species of orchids.
  • It’s also home to tribal communities like Santhals, Kolha, and Munda.

Best Time to Visit

November to mid-June is the best time to visit, with pleasant weather and better chances to spot wildlife. The park is closed during the monsoon season (June to October).

Getting There

Simlipal is accessible via Baripada (closest town), which is well-connected by road and rail. The nearest airport is in Bhubaneswar, around 270 km away.
