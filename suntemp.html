Konark Sun Temple, Odisha

About the Temple

The Konark Sun Temple, built in the 13th century by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga Dynasty, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This architectural marvel was designed as a colossal chariot for the Sun God, Surya, drawn by seven horses and supported on 24 intricately carved wheels.

The temple stands as an epitome of ancient Odisha's architectural brilliance and is admired for its intricate stone carvings, which depict deities, animals, dancers, and scenes from everyday life.

Interesting Facts

  • The 24 wheels of the temple are also sundials, capable of telling the time accurately.
  • The temple's magnetic structure was believed to cause ships to lose direction, hence called the "Black Pagoda" by European sailors.
  • Much of the main temple structure is in ruins, but the Jagamohana (assembly hall) still stands and showcases impressive architecture.
  • The Konark Dance Festival, held annually in December, celebrates India's classical dance heritage at this very site.

Best Time to Visit

October to February is the most pleasant time to visit Konark. The Konark Dance Festival during this period adds cultural vibrance to your trip.

Temple Timings

The monument is open daily from sunrise to sunset. Entry tickets can be booked online or at the gate.
